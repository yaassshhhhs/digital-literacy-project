# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist (For College Students in India)

## 🛡️ Cyber Safety Checklist

1. **Use strong and unique passwords** for every important account (email, banking, social media). Avoid using your name, DOB, or simple combinations.

2. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on Gmail, Instagram, LinkedIn, GitHub, and banking apps for extra protection.

3. **Never share OTPs or verification codes** with anyone—even if they claim to be from your bank, college, or customer support.

4. **Be cautious of suspicious links** received through WhatsApp, SMS, email, or Telegram claiming prizes, internships, or urgent account alerts.

5. **Verify internship and job offers** before applying or paying any registration fees. Genuine companies do not ask for upfront payments.

6. **Avoid using public Wi-Fi for financial transactions** such as banking, UPI payments, or logging into important accounts.

7. **UPI Safety Tip:** Always double-check the recipient’s name before approving any UPI payment request.

8. **UPI Safety Tip:** Never approve “Collect Request” payment notifications unless you personally initiated the transaction.

9. **Keep your devices updated** with the latest security updates and install apps only from trusted sources like Google Play Store or official websites.

10. **Log out from shared or public computers** after accessing your email, social media, or academic portals.

---

## 🚨 Reporting Cyber Crime in India

If you become a victim of cyber fraud or suspicious activity:

* Report immediately at **National Cyber Crime Portal:** [https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
* Call **Cyber Crime Helpline:** 1930

Early reporting increases the chances of recovering lost money and preventing further misuse.
