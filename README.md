# Task 1 – PRESENTATION

For this task, I created a one-page digital literacy awareness infographic using **Canva**. The infographic explains the meaning of digital literacy and why it is important for college students today. It highlights three key areas: understanding digital literacy, useful digital tools for students, and safe internet practices.

In the first section, I explained digital literacy as the ability to use digital tools responsibly, effectively, and safely for learning and communication. In the second section, I included useful student tools such as Google Drive for storage, GitHub for coding collaboration, and LinkedIn for building a professional profile. In the third section, I added safe internet practices such as using strong passwords, avoiding suspicious links, and protecting personal information online.

One interesting part of creating the infographic was learning how to organize information visually so that it looks simple and attractive while still being informative. The most challenging part was selecting the right layout and icons that clearly represented each idea.

# Task 4 – Professional Email & Etiquette Guide 

Poor digital communication can sometimes create misunderstandings between students and faculty or employers. For example, if a student sends an email without a subject line, greeting, or proper explanation, the professor may ignore the message or misunderstand the request. Similarly, sending informal messages to internship coordinators may reduce the chances of receiving a response.

This situation can be improved by writing clear subject lines, using respectful greetings, explaining the purpose of the email briefly, and ending with a professional sign-off. Following proper email etiquette helps maintain professionalism and improves communication effectiveness in academic and workplace environments.

# Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness Case Study & Prevention Guide 

While researching cybercrime awareness, I learned how common **UPI payment fraud and phishing scams** have become among college students. Many attackers trick users into sharing OTPs or approving fake payment requests by pretending to be bank representatives or offering fake rewards. I was surprised to see how quickly money can be transferred if someone approves a fraudulent collect request.
